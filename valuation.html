Our Valuation Methodologies

We use a combination of three core valuation standards to establish a robust, triangulated value range.

Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

We project future free cash flows (FCFF or FCFE) and discount them using a custom Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC), reflecting your specific risk profile and capital structure.

Market Comparables Analysis

We extract valuation multiples (e.g. EV/EBITDA, EV/Revenue, P/E) from transactional databases of completed private M&A deals and public market peers within your specific sector.

Asset-Based Valuation

We adjust the book value of your company's physical, financial, and intangible assets (such as intellectual property) to reflect current fair market rates, subtracting real liabilities.

When is a Certified Business Valuation Required?

  • Mergers & Acquisitions: Establish clear buy-side target parameters or sell-side baseline value expectations to optimize corporate negotiations.
  • IRS Estate & Gift Tax: Formulate defensible IRS valuations for corporate wealth transfer, asset gifting, and inheritance protection.
  • Partnership buyouts & disputes: Settle internal shareholder transitions and equity allocations with impartial, third-party reports.
  • Strategic Exit Planning: Identify enterprise value drivers and build growth targets years prior to marketing the firm.

Our Valuation Process

Phase 01

Information Sourcing & Review

Phase 02

Financial Modeling & Auditing

Phase 03

Certified Report Publishing
